Top Tax-Saving Investments & Deductions

Section 80C (₹1.5 Lakh Limit)

  • EPF/PPF contributions
  • ELSS mutual funds
  • Life insurance premium
  • NSC/Tax-saving FD
  • Home loan principal repayment
  • Children's tuition fees

Section 80D (Health Insurance)

  • Self & Family: ₹25,000
  • Parents (below 60): ₹25,000
  • Parents (senior citizens): ₹50,000
  • Preventive health checkup: ₹5,000
  • Maximum deduction: ₹1,00,000

HRA Exemption Calculator

Least of the following:

  • Actual HRA received
  • 50% of salary (metros) / 40% (non-metros)
  • Rent paid - 10% of salary

Other Important Deductions

  • Section 80E: Education loan interest
  • Section 80G: Donations (50-100% deduction)
  • Section 80TTA: Savings interest (₹10,000)
  • Section 24: Home loan interest (₹2 lakh)
  • Section 80EEA: Additional ₹1.5 lakh for first home

Which Tax Regime to Choose?

Choose Old Regime If:

  • Total deductions exceed ₹3.75 lakh annually
  • You have home loan (principal + interest)
  • High HRA component in salary
  • Multiple insurance policies and investments

Choose New Regime If:

  • Limited tax-saving investments
  • Income above ₹15 lakh (better tax slabs)
  • No home loan or HRA benefits
  • Prefer simplicity over deductions

Smart Tax-Saving Tips for 2025

  1. Maximize NPS Benefits: Additional ₹50,000 deduction under 80CCD(1B)
  2. Claim LTA: Travel within India for tax-free allowance
  3. Structure Salary: Optimize CTC with meal vouchers, travel allowance
  4. Joint Home Loan: Both co-owners can claim ₹2 lakh interest deduction
  5. Set off Losses: Carry forward capital losses for 8 years
  6. Gift to Family: Tax-free gifts to spouse/children for investments
Important Deadlines:
  • July 31, 2025: ITR filing for salaried individuals
  • March 31, 2025: Complete tax-saving investments
  • Quarterly: Advance tax payments for high earners
