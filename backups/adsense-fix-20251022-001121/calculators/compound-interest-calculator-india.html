How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel in Indian Rupees

1. Basic Compound Interest Formula in Excel

=Principal*(1+Rate/CompoundingFrequency)^(CompoundingFrequency*Years)

Example: ₹1,00,000 at 7% for 5 years (Monthly Compounding)

=100000*(1+0.07/12)^(12*5)

Result: ₹1,41,763.78

2. Compound Interest Formula Excel with Monthly Contributions

=FV(Rate/12, Years*12, -MonthlyContribution, -Principal)

Example: ₹1,00,000 initial + ₹5,000 monthly for 5 years at 7%

=FV(0.07/12, 5*12, -5000, -100000)

Result: ₹4,97,399.84

3. How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for 5 Years

Cell A1: Principal Amount = 100000 Cell A2: Annual Rate = 7% Cell A3: Years = 5 Cell A4: Compounding (Monthly) = 12 Cell A5: =A1*(1+A2/A4)^(A4*A3)

4. How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for Days

=Principal*(1+Rate/365)^Days

Example: ₹50,000 at 8% for 180 days

=50000*(1+0.08/365)^180

Result: ₹52,003.84

5. Creating a Compound Interest Table in Excel

Cell Formula/Value Description

6. Advanced Excel Formulas for Indian Investments

PPF Calculator (15 years, 7.1% compounded yearly)

=FV(0.071,15,-150000,0)

Fixed Deposit (Quarterly Compounding)

=Principal*(1+Rate/4)^(4*Years)

Recurring Deposit (Monthly Deposit)

=PMT_Amount*((1+Rate/4)^(Quarters)-1)/(1-(1+Rate/4)^(-1/3))

💡 Excel Tips for Indian Users:

  • Format cells as Indian currency: Right-click → Format Cells → Currency → Symbol: ₹
  • Use Indian number format: ##,##,##,##0.00
  • For lakhs/crores display: =TEXT(A1,"[>=10000000]##\\,##\\,##\\,##0;[>=100000]##\\,##\\,##0;##,##0")
  • Always use negative values for payments in FV function
  • Convert annual rate to monthly: Rate/12
  • Convert years to months: Years*12
