Detroit Property Tax System

Detroit property taxes are calculated based on the State Equalized Value (SEV) of your property, which is typically 50% of the true cash value. Property taxes fund essential city services including schools, police, fire protection, and infrastructure maintenance.

Current Detroit Property Tax Rates (2025)

  • City Operating Millage: 20.0 mills
  • School Operating Millage: 18.0 mills (DPSCD)
  • County Operating: 5.5 mills (Wayne County)
  • Library Millage: 4.6 mills
  • Parks & Recreation: 2.5 mills
  • Total Millage Rate: Approximately 50.6 mills

Note: Millage rates vary by district and can change annually. One mill equals $1 for every $1,000 of SEV.

How Detroit Property Taxes Are Calculated

  1. Assessed Value: City assessor determines market value
  2. State Equalized Value (SEV): Usually 50% of assessed value
  3. Taxable Value: SEV minus exemptions (if any)

Detroit Property Tax Exemptions

Detroit offers several property tax exemptions:

  • Homestead Exemption: Reduces school operating taxes for primary residences
  • Principal Residence Exemption: 18 mills reduction for owner-occupied homes
  • Poverty Exemption: For qualifying low-income homeowners
  • Disabled Veterans Exemption: For qualifying disabled veterans
  • Senior Citizen Exemption: For qualifying seniors with low income

Payment Options and Due Dates

  • Summer Tax Bill: Due July 31 (without penalty)
  • Winter Tax Bill: Due February 14 (without penalty)
  • Payment Plans: Available for qualifying taxpayers
  • Online Payments: Available 24/7 at DetroitMI.gov
  • In-Person: Coleman A. Young Municipal Center

Property Tax Appeal Process

If you believe your property is over-assessed, you can appeal:

  1. Board of Review: File by March 31 annually
  2. Michigan Tax Tribunal: Appeal Board of Review decisions
  3. Required Documentation: Recent sales, appraisals, or comparable properties

Consequences of Non-Payment

Important Warning

Unpaid property taxes can result in foreclosure. Detroit has an accelerated foreclosure timeline - properties can be foreclosed after just 3 years of delinquency.
