Understanding ROI

Return on Investment (ROI) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment. The formula is:

ROI = [(Final Value - Total Cost) / Total Cost] × 100

Common Use Cases:

  • Stock Investments: Calculate returns on stock market investments
  • Real Estate: Measure profitability of property investments
  • Business Projects: Evaluate project success and profitability
  • Marketing Campaigns: Measure advertising campaign effectiveness
  • Education: Assess the return on educational investments
💡 Tip: A positive ROI means profit, while a negative ROI indicates a loss. Generally, an ROI above 15-20% annually is considered good for most investments.
