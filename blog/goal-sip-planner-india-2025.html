Guide

Inflation-adjusted goal cost

FV = PV × (1 + infl)^years. Always plan for the inflated cost of your goal.

SIP formula (no step-up)

SIP = FV × (r/12) / ( (1 + r/12)^(12×years) − 1 )

Step-up SIP

Raising your SIP by ~5–10% yearly can lower the starting SIP. We approximate by simulating month-by-month with annual increases.

Example

₹10,00,000 goal today, 10 years, 12% return, 6% inflation → the calculator inflates the goal and solves the monthly SIP.

SIP vs Lumpsum

Lumpsum needs upfront capital but grows longer; SIPs are cash-flow friendly and reduce timing risk via rupee-cost averaging.

FAQs

How to calculate SIP for a goal?

Inflate the goal to its future value and apply the SIP formula using the monthly return. This page does it automatically.

How does inflation affect goal amount?

Higher inflation increases the future cost; plan accordingly using the FV = PV×(1+infl)^years formula.

What is step-up SIP?

An annual percentage increase to your SIP, helping keep pace with income growth and inflation.

How to find SIP for ₹1 crore goal?

Set goal today to ₹1,00,00,000 or use inflation to reach ₹1 crore in future, then compute required SIP.

Can SIP reach goals faster than Lumpsum?

Not faster, but SIPs are consistent and reduce timing risk. Lumpsum can outperform if invested early at good valuations.

What returns should I assume for equity SIPs?

Many planners use 10–12% p.a. long-term for diversified equity; adjust conservatively.
