Excel Formulas (Copy & Paste)

Use n for compounding frequency (Monthly=12, Quarterly=4, Yearly=1). Keep rate and periods consistent.

=FV(rate/n, years*n, -monthly, -principal, 0)
Example: =FV(10%/12, 10*12, -5000, -100000, 0)

Without contributions (only principal):

=FV(rate/n, years*n, 0, -principal, 0)
Example: =FV(10%/12, 10*12, 0, -100000, 0)

Step-up SIPs don't have a single built-in formula; model year-by-year with an increasing PMT (e.g., +5%/yr) or use this page's simulator.

Guide

Compound interest formula

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t), where P=principal, r=annual rate, n=compounds per year, t=years.

Translate to Excel

Use FV with consistent rate & periods: =FV(rate/n, years*n, -PMT, -PV, 0). Negative PMT/PV represent cash outflows.

Monthly contributions

With a monthly SIP, use n=12 or your desired frequency and set the monthly payment as PMT in FV.

Example (India)

Principal ₹1,00,000, 10 years, 10% p.a., monthly SIP ₹5,000, monthly compounding → =FV(10%/12, 10*12, -5000, -100000, 0).

FAQs

What is the Excel formula for compound interest?

Use =FV(rate/n, years*n, 0, -principal, 0); set n to 12/4/1 for monthly/quarterly/yearly.

How to include monthly contributions in Excel compound interest?

=FV(rate/12, years*12, -monthly, -principal, 0). Negative values follow the cash flow sign convention.

Which compounding frequency should I use in India?

Mutual funds can be modeled monthly (12). Fixed deposits often use quarterly (4) or yearly (1).

How do I calculate FUTURE VALUE in Excel with payments?

Use FV with PMT: =FV(rate/n, years*n, -PMT, -PV, 0).

What’s the difference between simple vs compound interest?

Simple interest doesn’t earn on interest; compound interest reinvests earnings and grows faster.

What errors to avoid in Excel compound interest calculations?

Keep rate & periods consistent, use negative signs for outflows, and don’t mix % with decimals.
