Recurring Deposit (RD) Formula for Indian Banks

RD Maturity Formula

Indian banks use a specific formula for RD calculations:

M = P √ó [(1 + r/4)^n - 1] / [1 - (1 + r/4)^(-1/3)]

Where P = Monthly Deposit, r = Annual Rate, n = Quarters

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: Which compound interest formula is used by Indian banks for FD?

Most Indian banks use quarterly compounding for Fixed Deposits. The formula is: A = P(1 + r/4)^(4t), where r is the annual rate and t is time in years.

Q2: How is TDS calculated on FD interest in Excel?

Use this formula: =IF(Interest>40000,(Interest-40000)*0.1,0) for regular citizens. For senior citizens, change 40000 to 50000.

Q3: What's the difference between FD and RD compound interest calculation?

FD involves a lump sum investment with compound interest, while RD involves monthly deposits with each deposit earning interest for different periods.

Q4: How to calculate effective yield after tax in Excel?

Effective Yield = ((Maturity - Principal - TDS) / Principal) * (100 / Years). This gives you the actual annual return after tax deduction.

Q5: Which Indian investment offers the best compound returns?

Currently, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (8.2%) and Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (8.2%) offer the highest government-backed returns with tax benefits.
