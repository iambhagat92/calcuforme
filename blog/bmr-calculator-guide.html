BMR Calculator Guide (Mifflin–St Jeor) – TDEE & Examples

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) estimates the calories your body needs at rest. The Mifflin–St Jeor equation is the current standard and, combined with an activity multiplier, gives Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

Male BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5 Female BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age − 161 TDEE = BMR × Activity (1.2–1.9)

Worked Example

Male, 30y, 175 cm, 70 kg, light activity (1.375): BMR ≈ 1649 kcal; TDEE ≈ 2266 kcal.

Tip: For gradual fat loss, target ~500 kcal/day below your TDEE; for muscle gain, ~250–500 kcal/day above.
