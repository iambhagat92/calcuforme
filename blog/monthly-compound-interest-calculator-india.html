FAQs: Monthly Compound Interest Calculator India

Q1: Which Indian banks offer monthly compound interest on FDs?

Most private banks like Kotak Mahindra, IndusInd Bank, and small finance banks offer monthly compounding. Public sector banks typically offer quarterly compounding. Always confirm with the bank before investing.

Q2: Is monthly compounding better than quarterly?

Yes, monthly compounding yields slightly higher returns. For a ‚Çπ10 lakh FD at 7% for 5 years, monthly compounding gives ‚Çπ14,17,625 vs ‚Çπ14,14,778 with quarterly compounding - a difference of ‚Çπ2,847.

Q3: How to calculate monthly compound interest for partial months?

Use this Excel formula: =Principal*(1+Rate/12)^(Months + Days/30). For 3 years 15 days at 7%: =100000*(1+0.07/12)^(36.5)

Q4: What's the TDS on FD interest in India?

Banks deduct 10% TDS if interest exceeds ‚Çπ40,000 (‚Çπ50,000 for senior citizens) in a financial year. Without PAN, TDS is 20%. You can submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if your income is below taxable limit.
