Michigan Home Buying Process: Step-by-Step

1

Check Credit & Save for Down Payment

Aim for a credit score of 620+ for conventional loans (580+ for FHA). Start saving for a 3-20% down payment plus closing costs (2-3% of home price).

Michigan Average: $215K home needs $6,450-$43,000 down payment
2

Get Pre-approved for Mortgage

Shop with 3+ Michigan lenders for the best rate. Consider MI Home Loan program for below-market rates if you qualify.

Top MI Lenders: Flagstar Bank, Chemical Bank, Lake Michigan Credit Union, Quicken Loans
3

Find a Michigan Real Estate Agent

Choose an agent familiar with Michigan markets, property taxes, and local programs. Look for experience in your target city.

Questions to Ask: Experience with MI programs? Knowledge of property tax assessments? Recent sales in area?
4

Home Search & Make Offer

Consider property taxes by city, school districts, and future development plans. Michigan's market moves fast in desirable areas.

Michigan Tip: Factor higher property taxes in Detroit vs. lower rates in suburbs when comparing homes
5

Home Inspection & Appraisal

Michigan homes need inspection for winter damage, foundation issues, and HVAC systems. Budget $300-500 for inspection.

Michigan Focus: Check for ice dam damage, furnace condition, basement moisture issues
6

Closing & Move In

Final walkthrough, sign documents, get keys! Michigan closing costs average 2-3% of home price. Don't forget homeowner's insurance.

Welcome Home! Register for homestead exemption to reduce property taxes

Common Michigan Home Buying Mistakes to Avoid

Underestimating Property Taxes

Property tax rates vary dramatically across Michigan. A $300K home in Detroit costs $5,100/year in taxes vs. $2,450 in Kalamazoo.

Ignoring Winter Costs

Budget extra for heating costs, snow removal, and potential weather damage. Michigan heating bills can be $200+ monthly in winter.

Skipping First-Time Programs

Michigan offers excellent first-time buyer programs. MI Home Loan can save you thousands with below-market rates and down payment assistance.

Not Shopping Lenders

Michigan has competitive lending markets. Shopping with just one lender could cost you thousands. Get quotes from at least 3 lenders.
