How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for Days: Daily Compounding Guide

Master the Excel formulas for calculating compound interest on a daily basis

Learning how to calculate compound interest in Excel for days is crucial for short-term investments, liquid funds, and any scenario where you need precise daily interest calculations. This guide shows you exactly how to use Excel formulas for daily compound interest, whether it's for 30 days, 90 days, or any specific number of days.

💡 When Daily Compounding Matters

Daily compounding is used in liquid funds, some savings accounts, and short-term FDs. The difference becomes significant for large amounts - ₹10 lakhs at 7% for 365 days gives ₹72,501 with daily compounding vs ₹70,000 with simple interest!

Excel Formula: How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for Days

📊 Daily Compound Interest Formula

A = P × (1 + r/365)^days
Where:
A = Final amount
P = Principal amount
r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
365 = Days in a year
days = Number of days for investment

Excel Implementation

Basic Formula:

=Principal*(1+AnnualRate/365)^Days

With cell references:

=A1*(1+B1/365)^C1

A1 = Principal, B1 = Annual Rate, C1 = Days

Practical Examples: Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for Days

Example 1: 90-Day Fixed Deposit

Principal: ₹5,00,000 | Rate: 6.5% p.a. | Period: 90 days

=500000*(1+0.065/365)^90

Result: ₹5,08,088.52

Interest Earned: ₹8,088.52

Step-by-Step: How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for Days

Step 1: Set Up Your Spreadsheet

Cell Label Example Value
A1 Principal Amount (₹) 500000
A2 Annual Interest Rate (%) 7.5
A3 Number of Days 45

Step 2: Apply the Daily Compound Interest Formula

In cell A5, enter:

=B1*(1+B2/100/365)^B3

This calculates the maturity amount with daily compounding

Step 3: Calculate Interest Earned

In cell A6, calculate interest:

=A5-B1

Step 4: Calculate Effective Annual Yield

To find annualized return:

=((A5/B1)^(365/B3)-1)*100

This shows the effective annual rate based on daily compounding

Advanced Daily Compound Interest Calculations

Leap Year Adjustment

For precise calculations in leap years:

=IF(MOD(YEAR(TODAY()),4)=0, Principal*(1+Rate/366)^Days, Principal*(1+Rate/365)^Days)

Date-Based Calculation

Calculate using actual dates:

=Principal*(1+Rate/365)^(EndDate-StartDate)

Example: A1=₹100000, B1=7%, C1=DATE(2025,1,1), D1=DATE(2025,3,15)

=A1*(1+B1/365)^(D1-C1)

FAQs: How to Calculate Compound Interest in Excel for Days

Q1: Should I use 365 or 360 days for banking calculations?

Most banks use 365 days (actual/365 convention). Some use 360 days for simplification. Check with your bank. In Excel: =Principal*(1+Rate/360)^Days for 360-day year.

Q2: How to calculate if investment spans multiple years?

Use total days directly. For 400 days: =Principal*(1+Rate/365)^400. Excel handles multi-year calculations automatically.

Q3: What's the formula for continuous compounding?

For theoretical continuous compounding: =Principal*EXP(Rate*Days/365). This gives the mathematical limit of infinite compounding frequency.
