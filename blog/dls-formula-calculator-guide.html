DLS Formula Calculator Guide: Par Score and Revised Target

The Duckworth–Lewis–Stern (DLS) method adjusts targets in rain-affected limited-overs matches by using resources: overs remaining and wickets in hand. This guide shows the core formula, terms, and a quick example so you can verify results from our DLS calculators.

DLS Formula

Target = floor(S × R2 / R1) + 1 Par Score = floor(S × R2 / R1)

Where:
S = Team 1 score
R1 = Team 1 resources used (%)
R2 = Team 2 resources available (%)

R1 and R2 Explained

R1 – Team 1 resources used
  • Calculated from overs faced and wickets lost in the 1st innings.
  • If the 1st innings is uninterrupted and full, R1 ≈ 100%.
R2 – Team 2 resources available
  • Remaining overs and wickets at the start/resumption of the chase.
  • Rain reductions or interruptions reduce R2 below 100%.

Worked Example

ODI match. Team 1 scores S = 250. First innings uninterrupted ⇒ R1 = 100%. Rain reduces second innings to 30 overs at start ⇒ R2 from DLS tables ≈ 75.1% (example).

Par = ⌊ 250 × 75.1 / 100 ⌋ = ⌊ 187.75 ⌋ = 187 Target = Par + 1 = 188

If Team 2 is 95/2 after 20 overs and another interruption leaves only 8 overs more, you must update R2 to the new remaining resources at resumption and recompute par/target.

FAQ

Do I need the full resource tables? Yes. For accurate results, use the ICC DLS resource tables to look up R1 and R2 based on overs remaining and wickets lost. Our calculators let you directly input those percentage values.

Multiple interruptions? Re-evaluate R2 at each resumption and apply the same formula.
