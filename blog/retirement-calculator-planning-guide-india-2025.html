‚ö†Ô∏è Common Retirement Planning Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Starting Too Late

Problem: Waiting till 35-40 to start retirement planning

Solution: Start in your 20s, even with small amounts (‚Çπ2,000-3,000). Power of compounding works best over long periods.

Mistake #2: Underestimating Inflation

Problem: Planning for today's expenses without accounting for inflation

Solution: Assume 7-8% inflation and calculate future expenses accordingly. What costs ‚Çπ50,000 today will cost ‚Çπ2,00,000 in 25 years.

Mistake #3: Over-Conservative Investing

Problem: Keeping all retirement savings in FDs/PPF (6-7% returns)

Solution: Maintain equity exposure (50-70%) till age 50. FD returns barely beat inflation - you need equity for real wealth creation.

Mistake #4: Ignoring Healthcare Costs

Problem: Not factoring in medical expenses which increase 10-15% annually

Solution: Get health insurance of ‚Çπ15-20 lakh and add 20% extra to retirement corpus for medical expenses.

Mistake #5: Withdrawing from Retirement Accounts Early

Problem: Breaking EPF/PPF/NPS for short-term needs

Solution: Keep separate emergency fund (6-12 months expenses) and never touch retirement corpus. Early withdrawal destroys compound growth.

Mistake #6: No Withdrawal Strategy

Problem: Not planning how to withdraw money in retirement

Solution: Plan systematic withdrawal starting 5 years before retirement. Keep 2-3 years of expenses in liquid funds always.

Mistake #7: Depending Only on EPF

Problem: Thinking EPF alone is sufficient for retirement

Solution: EPF is just 12-15% of retirement corpus. You need NPS, PPF, and equity mutual funds additionally.

üí∏ Retirement Withdrawal Strategy

Having a corpus is one thing, but withdrawing it smartly to last your entire retirement is equally important.

The 4% Withdrawal Rule (Adapted for India)

Understanding the 4% Rule

The 4% rule suggests withdrawing 4% of your retirement corpus in the first year, then adjusting for inflation each subsequent year. This ensures your corpus lasts 25-30 years.

Example:

  • Retirement Corpus: ‚Çπ3 crore
  • Year 1 Withdrawal: ‚Çπ12 lakh (4% of ‚Çπ3 crore) = ‚Çπ1 lakh/month
  • Year 2 Withdrawal: ‚Çπ12.72 lakh (adjusted for 6% inflation)
  • Year 3 Withdrawal: ‚Çπ13.48 lakh (adjusted for 6% inflation)

With 7-8% post-retirement returns, corpus should last 25-30 years

Smart Withdrawal Strategy

1Ô∏è‚É£ Bucket Strategy

  • Bucket 1 (2-3 years): Liquid funds - for immediate expenses
  • Bucket 2 (4-7 years): Debt funds - for medium-term needs
  • Bucket 3 (8+ years): Equity funds - for long-term growth

Refill Bucket 1 from Bucket 2 annually, and Bucket 2 from Bucket 3

2Ô∏è‚É£ Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)

  • Set up SWP from mutual funds for regular monthly income
  • Withdraw only gains initially, preserve capital
  • Tax efficient - only gains taxed, not entire withdrawal
  • Keep reviewing and adjusting withdrawal amounts

3Ô∏è‚É£ Annuity from NPS

  • At retirement, 40% of NPS corpus must go to annuity (monthly pension)
  • Annuity provides guaranteed monthly income for life
  • Choose annuity with spouse benefit and return of purchase price
  • 60% NPS corpus can be withdrawn (40% tax-free + 20% taxable)

Q6: Should I withdraw my EPF at retirement?

Answer: No, don't withdraw EPF immediately. Here's why:

  • EPF continues to earn interest: 8.25% per year even after retirement
  • Tax benefit: Interest is tax-free if withdrawn after retirement
  • Strategy: Keep EPF as emergency corpus and withdraw systematically over 5-10 years
  • Best practice: Withdraw monthly pension from NPS annuity + rental income, keep EPF untouched

Recommendation: Plan for 60 but build flexibility to retire earlier or work longer based on circumstances
