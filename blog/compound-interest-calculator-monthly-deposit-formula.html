Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Ignoring compounding frequency: Banks may use quarterly compounding for RDs
  2. Not accounting for taxes: Post-tax returns can be significantly lower
  3. Unrealistic return expectations: Use conservative estimates for planning
  4. Missing payments: Skipping deposits affects compounding significantly
  5. Not considering inflation: Real returns = Nominal returns - Inflation

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What is the formula for compound interest with monthly deposits?

The formula is: FV = PMT √ó [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r], where PMT is monthly payment, r is monthly rate, and n is total months.

Q2: How much will ‚Çπ5,000/month grow in 10 years at 12%?

‚Çπ5,000 monthly for 10 years at 12% annual return will grow to approximately ‚Çπ11,61,695, with total investment of ‚Çπ6,00,000.

Q3: Is SIP better than RD for monthly investments?

SIPs generally offer higher returns (12-15%) but with market risk. RDs offer guaranteed returns (6-7%) with no risk. Choose based on your risk appetite.

Q4: How to calculate step-up SIP returns?

For step-up SIPs, calculate each year's contribution separately with its compounding period, then sum all future values.

Q5: Which monthly investment gives best tax benefits?

ELSS SIP, PPF, and SSY offer Section 80C deductions. PPF and SSY returns are also tax-free, making them excellent for tax saving.
