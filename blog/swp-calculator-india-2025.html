Guide

What is SWP?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) lets you withdraw a fixed or increasing sum from your investments at regular intervals, creating a steady income stream.

Formula used

FV = PV*(1+r/12)^(12*t) − PMT*[(1+r/12)^(12*t) − 1]/(r/12)

We reverse the cash flow direction to simulate monthly withdrawals until the corpus depletes or reaches the selected horizon.

Inflation adjustment

When enabled, the withdrawal increases every month by the monthly inflation rate (inflation/12). You can also add an annual top-up percentage.

Safe withdrawal rate (~4%)

A conservative annual withdrawal near 3–4% of the corpus improves sustainability. Adjust based on risk, returns, and expenses.

SWP vs SIP

  • SIP: invests periodically to build wealth.
  • SWP: withdraws periodically to generate income from existing wealth.

FAQs

What is an SWP?

An SWP is a plan to withdraw a set amount from your investment at regular intervals, providing periodic income.

How does an SWP differ from SIP?

SIP invests regularly into funds; SWP withdraws regularly from your existing corpus.

How to calculate monthly withdrawals?

Each month, interest is added to the corpus, then your withdrawal is deducted. You can keep it fixed or grow it with inflation.

How long will my corpus last?

Depends on return, withdrawal, and inflation. The calculator simulates month-by-month until depletion or 100 years.

What happens if returns are lower?

Lower returns reduce interest earned, so the corpus can deplete sooner. Consider lowering withdrawals or increasing the corpus.

Is SWP good for retirement income?

Yes, if withdrawals are conservative and reviewed annually. Many retirees target around 3–4% per year.
