The 4% Withdrawal Rule

Safe Withdrawal Strategy

Withdraw only 4% of your corpus annually (adjusted for inflation) to ensure your money lasts 30+ years.

  • ₹5 Crore corpus = ₹20 lakh annual income (₹1.67 lakh/month)
  • ₹3 Crore corpus = ₹12 lakh annual income (₹1 lakh/month)
  • ₹2 Crore corpus = ₹8 lakh annual income (₹67,000/month)

Best Retirement Investment Options

1. Employee Provident Fund (EPF)

  • Current Rate: 8.25% tax-free
  • Contribution: 12% + 12% employer
  • Tax Benefit: Section 80C
  • Risk: Very Low

2. National Pension System (NPS)

  • Expected Returns: 9-12%
  • Extra Tax Benefit: ₹50,000
  • 60% lump sum, 40% annuity
  • Risk: Low to Moderate

3. Public Provident Fund (PPF)

  • Current Rate: 7.1% tax-free
  • Lock-in: 15 years
  • Maximum: ₹1.5 lakh/year
  • Risk: Zero (Government backed)

4. Equity Mutual Funds

  • Expected Returns: 12-15%
  • SIP for rupee cost averaging
  • Tax: 10% LTCG above ₹1 lakh
  • Risk: High (but reduces long-term)

Age-wise Retirement Planning Strategy

Age 25-35: Aggressive Growth Phase

  • 70% Equity (Mutual Funds, Stocks)
  • 20% Debt (EPF, PPF)
  • 10% Gold/Alternative investments
  • Focus: Maximum wealth creation

Age 35-45: Balanced Approach

  • 50% Equity
  • 40% Debt (Include NPS)
  • 10% Gold/REITs
  • Focus: Steady growth with stability

Age 45-55: Conservative Shift

  • 30% Equity
  • 60% Debt (FDs, Bonds, EPF)
  • 10% Gold
  • Focus: Capital preservation

Age 55-60: Pre-retirement Phase

  • 20% Equity (Large-cap only)
  • 70% Debt (Government bonds, FDs)
  • 10% Liquid funds
  • Focus: Income generation & safety

Common Retirement Planning Mistakes

⚠️ Avoid These Mistakes:

  1. Starting Late: Every 5-year delay doubles your required monthly investment
  2. Ignoring Inflation: ₹1 lakh today = ₹4.5 lakh after 25 years (6% inflation)
  3. No Health Insurance: Medical costs can wipe out retirement savings
  4. Over-dependence on Real Estate: Illiquid and maintenance heavy
  5. Not Reviewing Portfolio: Annual review and rebalancing essential

Action Plan: Start Today

Your Retirement Planning Checklist:

  1. Calculate your retirement corpus requirement
  2. Start SIP in 2-3 diversified equity funds
  3. Maximize EPF contributions (VPF if possible)
  4. Open NPS account for additional tax benefit
  5. Buy adequate term + health insurance
  6. Create emergency fund (6-12 months expenses)
  7. Review and rebalance portfolio annually
  8. Increase investments by 10% every year
Remember: The best time to start retirement planning was yesterday. The second best time is today. Even ₹5,000/month SIP started at 25 can create ₹2.3 crores by age 60!
