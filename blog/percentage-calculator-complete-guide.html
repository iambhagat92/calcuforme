What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" literally means "per hundred." Understanding percentages is crucial for everyday tasks like calculating discounts, interest rates, tips, and tax amounts.

Quick Tip: To convert any fraction to a percentage, multiply it by 100 and add the % symbol.

Basic Percentage Formulas

1. Finding What Percentage One Number is of Another

Formula: (Part / Whole) × 100 = Percentage

Example: What percentage is 25 of 200?
Solution: (25 / 200) × 100 = 12.5%

2. Finding a Percentage of a Number

Formula: (Percentage / 100) × Number = Result

Example: What is 20% of 500?
Solution: (20 / 100) × 500 = 100

3. Percentage Increase

Formula: [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100

Example: Price increased from ₹100 to ₹125. What's the percentage increase?
Solution: [(125 - 100) / 100] × 100 = 25%

4. Percentage Decrease

Formula: [(Old Value - New Value) / Old Value] × 100

Example: Price decreased from ₹200 to ₹150. What's the percentage decrease?
Solution: [(200 - 150) / 200] × 100 = 25%

Real-World Applications

Shopping Discounts

If a ₹5,000 item has a 30% discount:
Discount amount = (30 / 100) × 5,000 = ₹1,500
Final price = ₹5,000 - ₹1,500 = ₹3,500

Tax Calculations

Adding 18% GST to a ₹1,000 purchase:
GST amount = (18 / 100) × 1,000 = ₹180
Total amount = ₹1,000 + ₹180 = ₹1,180

Interest Rates

Simple interest on ₹10,000 at 5% for 1 year:
Interest = (5 / 100) × 10,000 = ₹500

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing percentage points with percentages: A change from 20% to 25% is a 5 percentage point increase, but a 25% relative increase.
  • Not using the correct base: Always identify what number represents 100% (the whole) in your calculation.
  • Forgetting to multiply by 100: When converting fractions to percentages, don't forget the final step.
  • Rounding too early: Keep extra decimal places during calculations and round only at the end.

Quick Percentage Tricks

10% trick: To find 10% of any number, just move the decimal point one place left. (10% of 450 = 45)
5% trick: Find 10% first, then divide by 2. (5% of 200 = 20 / 2 = 10)
1% trick: Move the decimal point two places left. (1% of 350 = 3.5)
50% trick: Simply divide by 2. (50% of 80 = 40)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert a decimal to a percentage?

Multiply the decimal by 100 and add the % symbol. For example, 0.75 = 75%

Can percentages be greater than 100%?

Yes! When something more than doubles, the percentage increase exceeds 100%. For example, growing from 50 to 150 is a 200% increase.

What's the difference between percentage and percentile?

Percentage is a fraction of 100, while percentile indicates the value below which a percentage of data falls. For example, being in the 90th percentile means you're above 90% of the group.
