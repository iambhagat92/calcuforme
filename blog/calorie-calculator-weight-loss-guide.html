Weight Loss Guide

Understanding Calories, BMR & TDEE

BMR is the energy your body needs at rest. TDEE multiplies BMR by your activity to estimate daily maintenance calories.

Creating a Calorie Deficit Safely

A common target is a 500 kcal/day deficit (≈0.45 kg/week). Prioritize protein, fiber, and whole foods to stay full.

How to Track Food Calories

Use nutrition apps or labels. Typical Indian foods: dal, roti, brown rice, curd, paneer, pulses, and vegetables.

Macro Split Example

P/C/F = 30/45/25. Adjust based on satiety and performance.

Sample 1800 kcal Indian Weight Loss Diet

  • Breakfast: Oats + Egg Whites
  • Lunch: Brown Rice + Dal + Veggies
  • Snack: Curd + Nuts
  • Dinner: Roti + Paneer + Salad

Exercise Recommendation

Do 30–45 minutes of brisk walking or cardio most days. Include strength training 2–3x/week if possible.

Water & Sleep Tips

Drink enough water (2–3L/day) and aim for 7–8 hours of sleep to support recovery and appetite control.

FAQs

How many calories should I eat to lose weight?

Use a ~500 kcal/day deficit from TDEE for steady, sustainable fat loss.

What is BMR and TDEE?

BMR is resting energy; TDEE includes activity. TDEE ≈ daily maintenance calories.

How to create a calorie deficit?

Reduce portion sizes, choose lean proteins, track intake, and increase daily activity.

How to calculate macros for fat loss?

A starting split of Protein 30%, Carbs 45%, Fat 25% works for many people.

How much weight can I safely lose per week?

About 0.25–0.75 kg/week is typical; ~0.45 kg/week at a 500 kcal/day deficit.

What are healthy Indian food options for weight loss?

Dal, roti, brown rice, veggies, curd, paneer, pulses, fruits, and nuts in sensible portions.
