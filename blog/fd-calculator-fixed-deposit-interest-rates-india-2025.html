üìê FD Calculation Formula

Simple Interest FD Formula

Maturity Amount = P + (P √ó r √ó t)

Where:

  • P = Principal amount
  • r = Annual interest rate (in decimal)
  • t = Tenure in years

Compound Interest FD Formula

A = P √ó (1 + r/n)^(n√ót)

Where:

  • A = Maturity amount
  • P = Principal
  • r = Annual rate (decimal)
  • n = Compounding frequency (4 for quarterly)
  • t = Tenure in years

üìã Types of Fixed Deposits

1. Regular Fixed Deposit

Standard FD with fixed tenure and interest rate. Flexible tenure from 7 days to 10 years. Premature withdrawal allowed with penalty.

2. Tax Saver FD (Section 80C)

5-year lock-in period. Get tax deduction up to ‚Çπ1.5 lakh under Section 80C. No premature withdrawal allowed. Interest taxable.

3. Cumulative FD

Interest is reinvested (compounded). Entire maturity amount paid at the end. Best for long-term wealth creation. Higher returns due to compounding.

4. Non-Cumulative FD

Interest paid out monthly/quarterly/annually. Best for regular income needs (retirees). Lower effective returns than cumulative FD.

5. Senior Citizen FD

Extra 0.25-0.50% interest for citizens above 60 years. Available at all banks. Best option for risk-free senior citizen investments.

6. Flexi FD (Sweep-In FD)

Linked to savings account. Automatically converts savings to FD above a limit. Flexible withdrawals. Best of both savings and FD.

üí∞ Tax on FD Interest

FD interest is fully taxable as "Income from Other Sources".

Important Tax Points

  • TDS Deduction: 10% TDS if interest > ‚Çπ40,000/year (‚Çπ50,000 for seniors)
  • No PAN = 20% TDS: Higher TDS without PAN
  • Form 15G/15H: Submit to avoid TDS if no tax liability
  • Tax Slab: Interest added to income, taxed as per your slab
  • No Indexation: Unlike debt mutual funds, no indexation benefit

Tax Calculation Example

Person in 30% Tax Bracket

  • FD Interest Earned: ‚Çπ50,000

  • Total Tax Liability: ‚Çπ15,000 (30% of ‚Çπ50,000)
  • Additional Tax at ITR: ‚Çπ10,000
  • Post-Tax Interest: ‚Çπ35,000

üí° FD Laddering Strategy

FD laddering helps you maintain liquidity while earning higher returns by staggering maturity dates.

Example: ‚Çπ5 Lakh FD Laddering

Instead of putting ‚Çπ5 lakh in one FD, divide into:

  • ‚Çπ1 lakh ‚Üí 1 year FD @ 7%
  • ‚Çπ1 lakh ‚Üí 2 year FD @ 7.25%
  • ‚Çπ1 lakh ‚Üí 3 year FD @ 7.5%
  • ‚Çπ1 lakh ‚Üí 4 year FD @ 7.25%

  • Emergency fund always available

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the minimum amount for FD?

Most banks allow FD from ‚Çπ1,000 minimum. Some banks like SBI allow from ‚Çπ100 for special FD schemes.

2. Can I withdraw FD before maturity?

Yes, premature withdrawal is allowed (except Tax Saver FD). Banks charge 0.5-1% penalty on interest rate. You get reduced interest rate for actual period held.

3. Which FD has highest interest rate?

Small finance banks offer highest rates (8-9%). Among major banks, HDFC and Axis Bank typically offer 7-7.5% for 2-3 year tenure.

4. Is FD better than savings account?

Yes, FD gives 6-8% vs savings account's 2.7-4%. However, FD has lock-in while savings account offers complete liquidity.

5. How is FD interest paid?

Two options: Cumulative (interest reinvested, paid at maturity) or Non-Cumulative (interest paid monthly/quarterly/annually). Cumulative gives higher returns.

6. What happens if bank fails?

DICGC (Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation) insures up to ‚Çπ5 lakh per depositor per bank including principal and interest.

7. Can I get loan against FD?

Yes, banks offer loans up to 90-95% of FD value at interest rates 1-2% above FD rate. No processing fees. Instant approval.

8. Which is better: Monthly or quarterly interest?

Quarterly compounding gives slightly higher returns than monthly interest payout. If you don't need regular income, choose cumulative FD with quarterly compounding.

9. Are FD returns guaranteed?

Yes, FD returns are 100% guaranteed. Interest rate is locked at time of booking. Not affected by market fluctuations.

10. FD vs RD - which is better?

FD for lump sum investment. RD (Recurring Deposit) for monthly savings. Both offer similar interest rates. FD gives higher maturity amount if you have lump sum available.

üéØ Conclusion: Is FD Right for You?

Fixed Deposits are perfect for risk-averse investors seeking guaranteed returns. While returns are moderate, the capital safety and predictability make FDs essential for financial planning.

Invest in FD If:

  • ‚úÖ You want 100% capital safety
  • ‚úÖ You need guaranteed returns
  • ‚úÖ You're saving for short-term goals (1-5 years)
  • ‚úÖ You're a senior citizen (get extra interest)
  • ‚úÖ You want regular income (non-cumulative FD)

Consider Alternatives If:

  • ‚ùå You want higher returns (try equity SIP)
  • ‚ùå You want tax-free returns (try PPF)
