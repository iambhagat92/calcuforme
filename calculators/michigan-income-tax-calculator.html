Michigan Income Tax Overview

Michigan has a flat income tax rate, making it one of the simpler state tax systems to understand. All Michigan residents and non-residents with Michigan income must file a Michigan income tax return if they meet certain income thresholds.

Michigan Tax Rates for 2025

  • Individual Income Tax Rate: 4.25% (flat rate)
  • Standard Deduction (Single): $5,000
  • Standard Deduction (Married Filing Jointly): $10,000
  • Personal Exemption: $5,000 per person
  • Dependent Exemption: $5,000 per dependent

Who Must File a Michigan Tax Return?

You must file a Michigan income tax return if you're:

  • A Michigan resident with income from any source
  • A part-year resident with Michigan income
  • A non-resident with Michigan source income
  • Filing a federal tax return and have Michigan income
  • Claiming a refund of Michigan taxes withheld

Michigan Tax Deductions and Credits

Available Deductions:

  • Standard deduction or itemized deductions
  • Personal and dependent exemptions
  • Retirement income deductions (age-based)
  • Military retirement pay exclusion

Common Tax Credits:

  • Homestead Property Tax Credit
  • Earned Income Tax Credit
  • Child and Dependent Care Credit
  • Michigan Education Credit
  • Charitable Contribution Credit

Michigan vs. Federal Tax Differences

Michigan tax law differs from federal tax law in several ways:

  • Some items taxable for federal purposes may not be taxable for Michigan
  • Different deduction amounts and rules
  • Separate exemption amounts
  • Different treatment of retirement income

Important Filing Dates

  • Filing Deadline: April 15, 2025 (same as federal)
  • Extension Deadline: October 15, 2025
  • Quarterly Estimates Due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15

Pro Tip

Michigan allows electronic filing and direct deposit for faster refunds. Most taxpayers receive their refunds within 2-3 weeks when filing electronically.
