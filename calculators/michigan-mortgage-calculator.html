Michigan Mortgage FAQ

What is the average mortgage rate in Michigan for 2025?

As of 2025, the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate in Michigan is approximately 6.75%. Rates can vary based on your credit score, down payment, loan type, and lender. Shop around with multiple Michigan lenders to get the best rate.

How much house can I afford in Michigan?

Generally, you can afford a home that costs 2.5-3 times your annual income. Your total monthly housing payment (PITI) should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income. With Michigan's median income of $59,584, you could typically afford a home around $150,000-$180,000.

What are Michigan property taxes like?

Michigan has relatively low property tax rates with an average of 1.27%, compared to the national average of 1.4%. However, rates vary significantly by county and city. Detroit has the highest at 2.04%, while rural areas can be as low as 0.8%.

Do I need PMI on a Michigan mortgage?

Yes, if you put down less than 20% on a conventional loan, you'll need Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). PMI typically costs 0.3% to 1.5% of the loan amount annually. You can remove PMI once you reach 20% equity in your home.

What first-time homebuyer programs are available in Michigan?

Michigan offers several programs through MSHDA: MI Home Loan (below-market rates), Down Payment Assistance (up to $7,500), and various local programs. These programs typically require homebuyer education and have income limits.
